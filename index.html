Enrolled in the Engineering Scholars Programme, I enjoy coding, computer-aided design as well as human-computer interaction. Over the years, I have had the opportunity to work on a number of incredible projects that have allowed me to grow and establish myself within this competitive industry. I hope that you will enjoy viewing my projects as much as I enjoyed working on them. Go ahead and explore, and do not hesitate to reach out if you would like to learn more!

Towards the end of the summer break, I formed a team of passionate engineering undergraduate students and participated in the NUS IDEATE 2021, a 3 day online ideathon organised by the NUS iDP Students' Club.

During the first half of 2021, I researched and authored a Policy Brief on the Inter-Agency Space Debris Coordination Committee (IADC) Space Debris Mitigation Guidelines Rev 5.7 to propose Space Debris Mitigation Policies for ASEAN Countries.

Over the 2021 summer break, I gathered a team of like-minded engineering undergraduate students to participate in the James Dyson Award, an international design award that celebrates, encourages and inspires the next generation of design engineers.
